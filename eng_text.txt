Anime is distinguished by a characteristic manner of drawing characters and backgrounds.
Today, anime represents a unique cultural layer.
The transfer of emotions in anime is a reason for a separate conversation.
Since anime series are usually shown on television at a frequency of about an episode per week.
To create them, a staff of a screenwriter, director, designer and several dozen animators are involved.
Today, images of characters are worked out down to the smallest details of their face and demeanor.
Hair in anime usually consists of strands.
For example, red hair is a characteristic indicator of temper.
